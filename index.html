Introduction to Nursing Informatics

Introduction to Nursing Informatics provides a foundational understanding of the field, covering its principles, history, and interdisciplinary nature. It explores how nursing science, computer science, and healthcare converge to enhance patient care through technology. Topics include EHRs, CDSS, telehealth, and ethical considerations. This course equips nurses to leverage information technology for evidence-based practice and improved patient outcomes.

The History of Nursing Informatics

The history of nursing informatics is a fascinating journey that has evolved alongside advancements in technology and healthcare delivery systems. Here's an overview:

1960s-1970s: Early Beginnings
The roots of nursing informatics can be traced back to the 1960s when computers started to gain prominence in various industries, including healthcare.

1980s: Formalization of the Field
The 1980s marked the formalization of nursing informatics as a distinct specialty within the nursing profession.

1990s: Adoption of Electronic Health Records
The 1990s saw a significant shift towards the adoption of electronic health records (EHRs) in healthcare settings.

2000s: Integration of Technology into Nursing Practice
With the rapid advancement of technology, nursing informatics expanded beyond basic EHR systems to encompass a wide range of digital tools and applications.

2010s-Present: Embracing Digital Transformation
The 2010s witnessed a continued embrace of digital transformation in healthcare, driven by trends such as big data, artificial intelligence, and the Internet of Things (IoT).
